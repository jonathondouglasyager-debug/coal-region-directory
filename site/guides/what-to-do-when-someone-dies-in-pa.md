## What to Do When Someone Dies in Pennsylvania

Losing someone you love is overwhelming. This guide walks you through the practical steps — not to replace the support of a funeral director, but to help you understand what comes next.

### In the First Hours

**If the death occurs at home:**
- If the person was under hospice care, call the hospice nurse. They will pronounce the death and guide you through next steps.
- If the death was unexpected, call 911. The police and coroner may need to respond.
- You do not need to rush. Take the time you need.

**If the death occurs in a hospital or care facility:**
- The staff will handle the immediate medical and legal steps.
- They will ask you to choose a funeral home. If you don't have one in mind, ask for time to decide.

### Choosing a Funeral Home

In Schuylkill County, every funeral home is locally owned. You can choose any funeral home — you are not required to use the closest one or the one suggested by a hospital.

Things to consider:
- **Location** — a funeral home close to your community may be more convenient for family and friends
- **Services offered** — cremation, burial, or both
- **Reputation** — ask neighbors and family for their experiences
- **Pre-existing arrangements** — check if the deceased made pre-arrangements with a specific provider

### In the First Few Days

Your funeral director will guide you through most of these, but it helps to know what's ahead:

1. **Death certificate** — The funeral home typically files this with the county. You'll need 10-15 certified copies for insurance, banks, and legal matters. In Schuylkill County, certificates are filed through the PA Department of Health.

2. **Obituary** — Your funeral director can help draft and place this in local papers like the Republican Herald or the Pottsville Free Press.

3. **Funeral or memorial arrangements** — Decide on visitation, service, burial or cremation, and any religious or cultural traditions.

4. **Notify key people** — Employer, close friends, church or community organizations, social security (1-800-772-1213).

### In the First Few Weeks

- **Locate important documents** — will, life insurance policies, bank accounts, property deeds
- **Contact the attorney** if there's a will or estate to settle
- **Notify government agencies** — Social Security, VA (if veteran), pension providers
- **Update or cancel accounts** — utilities, subscriptions, credit cards
- **File life insurance claims** — you'll need death certificates for each claim

### Pennsylvania-Specific Notes

- Pennsylvania does not require embalming unless the body is transported across state lines or the funeral is delayed beyond a certain period.
- PA inheritance tax applies to most estates. Rates vary: 0% for surviving spouses, 4.5% for lineal descendants, 12% for siblings, 15% for others.
- The Schuylkill County Register of Wills handles probate. Their office is in the county courthouse in Pottsville.

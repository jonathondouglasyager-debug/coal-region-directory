## Pre-Planning Your Funeral: A Checklist

Pre-planning your funeral is one of the most thoughtful things you can do for your family. It ensures your wishes are honored and removes the burden of decision-making from grieving loved ones.

Nearly every funeral home in Schuylkill County offers pre-planning services — most with online forms you can start from home.

### Why Pre-Plan?

- **Your wishes are documented.** No guesswork for your family about burial vs. cremation, religious traditions, or music choices.
- **Costs can be locked in.** Some providers offer guaranteed pricing when you pre-pay.
- **It reduces stress.** Families who've already pre-planned consistently report less anxiety during the grieving process.
- **You have time to compare.** Without the pressure of an immediate need, you can visit multiple providers, ask questions, and make informed decisions.

### The Checklist

#### 1. Basic Decisions
- [ ] Burial or cremation?
- [ ] Traditional funeral service, memorial service, or celebration of life?
- [ ] Religious or secular ceremony?
- [ ] Open or closed casket (if burial)?
- [ ] Visitation / viewing — yes or no? How many days?

#### 2. Location Preferences
- [ ] Which funeral home?
- [ ] Church or chapel service?
- [ ] Which cemetery (if burial)?
- [ ] Reception or gathering location after the service?

#### 3. Personal Touches
- [ ] Music selections
- [ ] Readings, poems, or scripture
- [ ] Who should deliver the eulogy?
- [ ] Photos or displays
- [ ] Charitable donations in lieu of flowers?
- [ ] Military honors (if veteran)?

#### 4. Financial Planning
- [ ] Get price lists from 2-3 funeral homes (they're required by law to provide these)
- [ ] Decide whether to pre-pay or simply document wishes
- [ ] Consider preneed insurance or a funeral trust
- [ ] Designate who has authority to carry out your plans

#### 5. Documents to Gather
- [ ] Birth certificate
- [ ] Social Security number
- [ ] Marriage certificate
- [ ] Military discharge papers (DD-214) if applicable
- [ ] Life insurance policies
- [ ] Cemetery deed (if you own a plot)
- [ ] Will and estate documents

#### 6. People to Notify
- [ ] Create a list of people to be contacted
- [ ] Include phone numbers, emails, and relationships
- [ ] Note anyone who should be a pallbearer
- [ ] Identify your next of kin / decision-maker

## Veterans Burial Benefits in Pennsylvania

Schuylkill County has a proud tradition of military service. If your loved one served in the U.S. Armed Forces, several benefits may be available to help with burial and memorial costs.

### Federal VA Burial Benefits

**Burial Allowance**
The VA provides a burial allowance to help cover costs:
- **Service-connected death:** Up to $2,000 toward burial expenses
- **Non-service-connected death (VA hospital):** Up to $948 for burial and up to $948 for plot
- **Non-service-connected death (other):** Up to $948 for burial if receiving VA pension/compensation

**Government Headstone or Marker**
- Available at **no cost** for any eligible veteran, regardless of burial location
- Includes inscription of name, branch of service, dates of birth and death
- Flat markers, upright headstones, and niche covers available
- Can also be placed in private cemeteries — the VA ships directly

**Presidential Memorial Certificate**
- A certificate signed by the current President, honoring the veteran's service
- Available to family members at no cost

**Burial Flag**
- A United States flag is provided to drape the casket, then given to next of kin
- Available at no cost from the VA or most local post offices

### National and State Cemeteries

**Indiantown Gap National Cemetery**
- Located in Lebanon County, approximately 45 minutes from Pottsville
- Open to eligible veterans, spouses, and dependent children
- No cost for the gravesite, opening/closing, or government headstone
- Provides military funeral honors

**Pennsylvania State Veterans Cemeteries**
- Additional state-run options available across Pennsylvania
- Contact the PA Department of Military and Veterans Affairs for eligibility

### Military Funeral Honors

Every eligible veteran is entitled to a military funeral honors ceremony, which includes:
- Folding and presenting the burial flag
- Playing of Taps (live or recorded)
- At least two uniformed service members

Your funeral director can help coordinate military honors. Local VFW and American Legion posts in Schuylkill County often provide honor guard services.

### How to Apply

1. **Gather the DD-214** (Certificate of Release or Discharge from Active Duty). If you can't find it, request a copy from the National Personnel Records Center.
2. **Contact a funeral home** — Most Schuylkill County funeral directors are experienced with VA claims and can file on your behalf.
3. **File VA Form 21P-530** for the burial allowance, or ask your funeral director to submit it.
4. **For headstones**, file VA Form 40-1330 — your funeral director or cemetery can help.

### Local Resources

- **Schuylkill County Veterans Affairs Office** — Located in the county courthouse in Pottsville. They can help with benefit claims and connecting to services.
- **Local VFW and American Legion Posts** — Many posts offer assistance with funeral arrangements and honor guard details.

### Important Notes

- Benefits must be applied for — they are not automatic.
- Keep multiple copies of the DD-214 in a safe, accessible place.
- Spouses and dependent children of eligible veterans may also qualify for burial in a national cemetery.
